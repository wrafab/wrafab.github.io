FINAL PROJECT

The production of your final project must utilize at least four different pieces of equipment and/ or significantly different process. It is highly recommended you discuss your project idea in advance with me to get permission for your final project. The preferred final project will involve four different pieces of equipment, but it is possible to submit multiple projects to meet this criteria. All final projects must be original in creation and not created by anyone else. It the work is not original, it will be considered a violation of our Honor Code. The project will be assessed based upon the quality, complexity, processes used, aestetics and functionality. You must create a separate page that documents how the project was created with multiple pictures documenting each step of the process. The page should model an Instructables page. YOU CANNOT HAVE A T-SHIRT AS YOUR FINAL PROJECT!


Assignment

1. Describe your project.

2. What equipment and materials did you use to create the project.

3. How much time did you spend working on the project?

4. What settings and configurations did you use on each machine.

5. What did you learn from this project?

6. How much time did you spend working on the project.

7. Please document each step of your project including photographs and include ALL project files.

This is due during your last class meeting of the course. You will present for a few minutes on your project.
