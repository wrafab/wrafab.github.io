3D Printing

additive vs subtractive vs. Formative processes

Formative - high volume production, high initial investment in mold by then produce at low unit price

  • Injection Molding
  • Casting
  • Stamping and Forming

Subtractive - best for simple geometry parts that are generally made from metals at middle unit price

  • CNC (Computer Numerically Controlled)
  • Turning
  • Drilling

Additive - low volume, complex designs that formative or subtractive methods are unable to produce or a one off rapid prototype is required

  • 3D Printing
